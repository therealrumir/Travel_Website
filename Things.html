Things to Do in Agra

Agra is a very diverse city. It has many different experiences to offer. This experiences can be done by you in Agra and this list tells you what you can do. You can plan these experiences with the categories listed below.

Food

  • Peethe Tasting - You can taste Peetha which originated from Shah Jahan's royal kitchens. You can sample this delicasy in the Agra markets.
  • Mughlai Cuisine - You can taste the Mughlai culture like Tandoore Chicken and Tikka Masala. You can do this ate famous restaurants like Pind Balluchi and Peshawri.
  • Street Food - You can experience the blasting flavours and food scenes at the Sadar Bazaar.Some famous street food options are dahi bhalla and aloo tikki.
  • Rooftop Dining - You can enjoy rooftop meals with a view of the Taj Mahal. Some famous restaurants where you can experience this experience at Taj Terrace and Tea'se Me.

These food experiences can be experienced at:

  • Pinch of Spice
  • Joney's Place
  • Mama Chicken Mama Franky house
  • Taj Bano

Itinerary

This is a simple itinerary for a one day trip to Agra:

  1. Sunrise (5:30 AM - 8:30 AM): The Taj Mahal - Arrive at the Taj Mahal before sunrise to witness the white marble monument change colors with the rising sun. This is the calmest time of day with the fewest crowds. Book tickets online in advance to skip the queues.
  2. Morning (9:00 AM - 11:30 AM): Agra Fort - Head to the nearby Agra Fort, a massive red sandstone fortress that was the main residence of the Mughal emperors. Explore its palaces, audience halls (Diwan-i-Aam and Diwan-i-Khas), and enjoy the view of the Taj Mahal from the Musamman Burj tower, where Shah Jahan spent his final years imprisoned
  3. Lunch (11:30 AM - 1:00 PM): Local Cuisine - Enjoy lunch at a local restaurant. Agra is renowned for its Mughlai cuisine and local specialties like petha (a sweet made from ash gourd) and dalmoth (a spicy snack).
  4. Afternoon (1:00 PM - 2:30 PM): Tomb of Itimad-ud-Daulah - Visit the exquisite Tomb of Itimad-ud-Daulah, often called the "Baby Taj". This marble mausoleum features intricate pietra dura (inlay work) and is considered a precursor to the Taj Mahal. It's generally less crowded than the main attractions.
  5. Late Afternoon (3:00 PM - 4:30 PM): Mehtab Bagh - Walk through Mehtab Bagh, a square-shaped garden complex across the Yamuna River. It offers a stunning, symmetrical view of the Taj Mahal, perfect for photos, especially as the sun begins to set behind it.
  6. Evening (4:30 PM onwards): Shopping and Departure - Explore the vibrant local markets like Kinari Bazaar or Sadar Bazaar for handicrafts, leather goods, and souvenirs. This is a good time to head back to your hotel or the train station for departure.
